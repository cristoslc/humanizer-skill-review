## Detect Mode — Issues Found

### P0 — Credibility Killers
- **"In today's rapidly evolving digital landscape"** — dead giveaway opener. No one talks like this.
- **"the pen is mightier than the sword, but only when wielded with intention"** — cliché with a tacked-on twist. The twist doesn't add information; it just signals "I know this is a cliché so I'm subverting it."

### P1 — Obvious AI Smell
- **"it's important to note that"** — Tier 1 filler. Says nothing.
- **"Let's dive into"** — template transition opener.
- **"It's not just about stringing words together; rather, it's about crafting a narrative that resonates"** — "It's not X; it's Y" construction.
- **"they craft, they hone, they iterate"** — compulsive rule of three. Also contains em dashes (—).
- **"This trifecta of habits"** — self-labeling significance. The label does the work the content should do.

- **"In conclusion"** — generic conclusion opener.
- **"mastering communication is a journey, not a destination"** — cliché.
- **"By embracing these principles, you'll be well-equipped to navigate the complexities of modern writing"** — generic closing template.
- **"make or break your success"** / **"separates good from great"** — cliché pair.

### P2 — Stylistic Polish
- **Uniform paragraph length** — all four paragraphs are 3–4 sentences. No variation.
- **Formulaic structure** — generic opener → "let's dive" → "it's worth noting" → "in conclusion." The piece is a template, not an argument.
- **Paragraph-reshuffle immunity** — swap any two body paragraphs and nothing breaks. It's a list, not a progression.
- **Em dashes** — one instance in "they craft, they hone, they iterate —" (the em dash after "write" before "they craft").

### Assessment
Nearly every flag here is a **clear problem** — the text is a textbook example of generic AI blog prose. The only judgment call is the cliché-with-twist closing line, which a human *could* write intentionally, but in context it reads as another template move. The piece has zero concrete claims, zero named references, zero data points — it's all filler.

### Ethics Note
These are signals, not proof. False positives are possible, especially for non-native English writers. The patterns flagged here are statistically more common in LLM output, but humans writing under deadline pressure or in an unfamiliar genre produce the same shapes. Pair the signal with context before acting on it.
