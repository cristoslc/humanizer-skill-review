**2. Rewritten version**

Writing well matters more than most people think. Not because the words are fancy, but because the structure carries the reader somewhere.

Good writers do three things. They cut the fluff. They read their drafts out loud. And they kill their darlings — the clever lines that don't actually serve the point. That's it. That's the whole secret.

Structure is the part everyone skips. A strong opening sets a direction. Each paragraph moves the reader one step forward. The conclusion lands on something specific — not a platitude, but a real takeaway. If a paragraph doesn't move the argument forward, cut it.

The clichés about writing are true: you get better by writing badly, often, and then fixing it. The pen is a tool, not a weapon. Use it to say one thing clearly instead of ten things vaguely.
