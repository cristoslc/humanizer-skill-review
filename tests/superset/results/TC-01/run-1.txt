## 2. Rewritten version

Good writing is hard. Most people skip the hard part.

Structure matters more than most people think. A clear structure does two things: it holds your reader's attention and it makes your point land. The opening line sets the hook. Each paragraph builds on the last. The ending closes the loop. That's it — no magic.

Write like you mean it. Cut the filler. Say what you want to say and stop.

## 3. What changed

- Cut the opener entirely — "In today's rapidly evolving digital landscape" is pure filler
- Replaced the "Let's dive into" transition with a direct statement
- Removed all three negative parallelisms ("it's not X, it's Y")

- Replaced "serves as a building block" with "does two things"
- Cut "In conclusion" and the cliché about journeys and destinations
- Replaced the generic ending with a concrete, short closing

## 5. Ethics note

These are signals, not proof. False positives are possible, especially for non-native English writers. The patterns flagged here are statistically more common in LLM output, but humans writing under deadline pressure or in unfamiliar genres produce the same shapes. Don't use this as the sole basis for a consequential decision.
