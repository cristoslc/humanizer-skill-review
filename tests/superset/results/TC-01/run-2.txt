## 1. Issues found

**P0 — Credibility killers**
- None present (no cutoff disclaimers, chatbot artifacts, unfilled placeholders, etc.)

**P1 — Obvious AI smell**
- **"In today's rapidly evolving digital landscape"** — Tier 1 template opener. Every word is padding.
- **"it's important to note that"** — Tier 1 filler phrase. Adds nothing.
- **"more critical than ever"** — Significance inflation on a generic claim.
- **"Whether you're a seasoned professional or just starting out"** — Slot-fill binary. Formulaic.
- **"Let's dive into"** — Tier 1 transition template.
- **"It's not just about...; rather, it's about..."** — "It's not X, it's Y" construction.
- **"they craft, they hone, they iterate"** — Compulsive rule of three.
- **"This trifecta of habits"** — Self-labeling significance + inflated vocabulary.
- **"what separates good from great"** — Tier 1 template phrase.
- **"It's worth noting that"** — Tier 1 filler phrase.
- **"A well-structured piece doesn't just inform; it guides the reader on a journey"** — Second "it's not X, it's Y" construction.
- **"From the hook to the conclusion"** — Formulaic range construction.
- **"In conclusion"** — Generic transition.
- **"mastering communication is a journey, not a destination"** — Cliché.
- **"By embracing these principles, you'll be well-equipped to navigate the complexities"** — Inflated, generic.
- **"the pen is mightier than the sword, but only when wielded with intention"** — Cliché with a twist, still a cliché.

**P2 — Stylistic polish**
- **Uniform paragraph length** — all three body paragraphs are 3 sentences.
- **Formulaic openings** — every paragraph starts with a template phrase.
- **Zero concrete details** — no numbers, names, dates, or examples.
- **One em dash** — in "they craft, they hone, they iterate — they craft"

## 2. Rewritten version

Effective communication can make or break your career. Whether you're a CEO or an intern, the way you write shapes how people see you.

Writing well takes work. The best writers don't just put words on a page. They revise, cut, and restructure until the piece flows. That iterative process is what makes the difference.

Structure matters more than most people think. A strong hook pulls the reader in. Each paragraph moves the argument forward. The conclusion ties it together. Every piece needs that architecture.

Keep practicing. Keep editing. Writing is a skill you build over time, not something you're born with.

## 5. Ethics note

These are signals, not proof. False positives are possible, especially for non-native English writers. The patterns flagged here are statistically more common in LLM output, but humans writing under deadline pressure or in unfamiliar genres produce the same shapes. Don't use this analysis as the sole basis for a consequential decision.
