## 2. Rewritten version

Good communication matters more than most people realize. Not because the tools are fancy, but because the stakes are real. A poorly worded email costs you a client. A sharp paragraph wins one.

Structure is the difference. A strong opening pulls the reader in. Each paragraph builds on the last. The conclusion lands because the path there was clear, not because you announced you were concluding.

The best writers I know share one habit: they revise. Not once, not twice — until the thing says what they actually mean. That's it. No trifecta, no secret sauce. Just the willingness to cut what doesn't work.

If you take anything from this, take that. Write, then rewrite. The rest takes care of itself.

## 5. Ethics note

These are signals, not proof. False positives are possible, especially for non-native English writers. The patterns flagged above are statistically more common in LLM output, but humans writing under deadline pressure or in unfamiliar genres produce the same shapes. Pair the signal with context.
